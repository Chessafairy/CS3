Axolotl morphs

Leucistic Axolotls

Leucistic Axolotls are a variant of axolotls that have translucent white skin, crimson or pink gills, and dark brown or black eyes together with dazzling gold specks. They are one of the most prevalent and attractive variants in captivity, but they are quite rare in the wild since predators can easily recognize them.

Wild type Axolotls

Wild type Axolotls are a variant of axolotls that have mottling that is dark grayish green and black. They also have a pale belly and iridophores that have gold flecks in them. They have the same hues and patterns as wild species do. he pet industry has used this shade the longest, and it is the standard. In 1863, the first species of the wild were brought to Europe.
